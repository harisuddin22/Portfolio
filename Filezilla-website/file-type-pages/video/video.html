Video Formats

MP4: This is a widely accepted format for web streaming, playback, and video sharing due to its compatibility and balance of file size and quality. It uses the H.264 or H.265 codec to compress video data and the MP3 or AAC codec to compress audio data.

MOV: This is a format developed by Apple, commonly used for storing high-quality video files and frequently used in professional video editing. It uses the H.264 codec to compress video data and various codecs to compress audio data.

AVI: This is an older format developed by Microsoft, used for playing video on Windows devices. It can use various codecs to compress video and audio data, but it can be large in size.

MKV: This is an open-source format that can store a wide range of video, audio, and subtitle tracks in one file. It can use various codecs to compress video and audio data, but it may not be compatible with some devices or players.

WebM: This is a format designed for the web, it offers good compression and video quality. It uses the VP8, VP9, or AV1 codec to compress video data and the Vorbis or Opus codec to compress audio data.

MPEG-2: This is a format used for DVDs and sometimes for TV broadcasts. It uses the MPEG-2 codec to compress video data and various codecs to compress audio data.
